Your Macs display

as a light source

 

Simple really.

Your iPhone is the remote control.

Your Macs' display is the light source.

Just open the app on your Mac.

The iPhone will instantly recognize it

if they're on the same Wi-Fi network.

 

No need to mess around with IP addresses or port numbers.

 

The iPhone and your Macs will recognize themselves and connect automatically.

 

RoomColors for your Mac lives in the status bar, and doesn't get in your way.

 

 

This application is a concept

for a remote controlled

lighting environement

 

The application illustrates the practicality of using your iPhone to control the lighting in your home. Ideally you'd have some sort of WI-Fi connected light bulbs. But I'm using the Macs displays as subtitutes for light bulbs.

 

You pick the color to be displayed full screen on your Mac

and adjust the brightness of the display.

 

What to use it for?

 

You can use it as a mood light.

Create the mood you want in your room by lighting it with a certain color.

 

You can use it as a soft box for photography
